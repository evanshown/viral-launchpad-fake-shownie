# Shown Media — Single-Page Site

A loud, retro/Y2K-internet landing page for Shown Media: the NYC viral marketing agency for DTC brands and UGC. One scrolling page, no extra routes.

## Visual direction — Retro / Y2K internet
- **Vibe:** late-90s/early-2000s web nostalgia meets modern polish. Playful, not chaotic.
- **Palette:** electric lime, hot magenta, cyber blue, off-white cream background, deep ink black for type. High contrast, candy-colored.
- **Type:** chunky display font for headlines (Space Grotesk / monospace mix), clean sans for body. Oversized hero type.
- **Texture:** subtle dotted/grid backgrounds, sticker-style badges, marquee scrollers, dashed borders, drop-shadow buttons with classic offset-block shadow.
- **Motion:** horizontal marquee tickers, hover wiggle/tilt on stickers, animated underlines, cursor-reactive accents on the hero.

## Page sections (top → bottom)

1. **Top nav bar**
   - Shown Media wordmark left, anchor links (Services, Proof, Contact), chunky "Get Shown" button right.
   - Sticky, with a thin marquee strip above it: "NYC • $3M → $12M ARR • 66 humans • now booking Q3 launches •" looping.

2. **Hero**
   - Massive headline: "We turn companies into internet moments."
   - Sub: "NYC-based viral marketing & content for DTC brands. Paid ads, launches, and UGC that actually gets seen."
   - Two CTAs: "Get Shown" (primary, sticker-style) + "See the work" (ghost).
   - Floating retro stickers/badges around the type ("⚡ viral", "🚀 launches", "📈 scale", "🗽 NYC").

3. **Stats strip**
   - Four big numbers in a row: **$12M ARR**, **66 team**, **4× growth in months**, **NYC HQ**. Each in a colored sticker tile with offset shadow.

4. **Three pillars — Services**
   - Section header: "What we do."
   - Three large cards side-by-side (stacked on mobile):
     - **Paid Ads** — "Targeted ads that turn viewers into customers, at any scale."
     - **Viral Launches** — "End-to-end product launches: scripting, on-site production, distribution."
     - **UGC** — "Creator-led content built to perform across feeds and formats."
   - Each card: bold icon/sticker, title, 2-line description, hover state with color flip.

5. **Founder / why us blurb**
   - Short paragraph framed like a sticky note or terminal window: Matthew Epstein's story (built a YouTube channel to 3M subs by 17, worked with major tech brands, founded Shown Media). One punchy paragraph.

6. **Testimonials — Social proof**
   - Section header: "What people say."
   - 4–6 testimonial quotes in a staggered/collage layout (Polaroids or sticky notes pinned at slight angles). Placeholder quotes from "Founder, [DTC Brand]" — easy to swap real ones in later.
   - Optional second marquee row of short pull-quotes scrolling.

7. **Big CTA block**
   - Full-width colored band: "Ready to get shown?" + inline form (name, brand, email, what you're launching) with a toast confirmation on submit. Front-end only for now.

8. **Footer**
   - Wordmark, NYC tag, social links (placeholders: IG, TikTok, X, LinkedIn, YouTube), tiny "© Shown Media" line, one more marquee for fun.

## Notes
- Fully responsive — stickers and marquees adapt cleanly on mobile.
- All copy drawn from what you shared; easy to edit later.
- No backend, no auth, no extra pages — pure marketing site.
- Form is front-end only with a success toast. If you later want submissions to go to email/Slack/a database, we can add Lovable Cloud then.
